# Concept & Brand Philosophy

## Core Idea

The site should feel like a **premium paper invitation with a wax seal — translated into the web**.
Not a simulation of paper. Not a scrapbook aesthetic. A *translation*: the same emotional weight,
the same tactile intentionality, the same sense of ceremony — expressed through pixels and interaction.

Every decision must pass this test:
> "Would this detail exist on a ₽5,000 printed invitation?"

If yes — include it, execute it perfectly.
If no — cut it.

---

## Design Pillars

### 1. Restraint over decoration
Negative space is the primary design element. Do not fill gaps with ornaments.
A heading floating in silence carries more weight than one surrounded by flourishes.

### 2. Precision over abundance
One perfectly placed 0.5px divider is worth more than five decorative borders.
Every visual element must earn its place.

### 3. Warmth without kitsch
The Squirrel palette is earthy and deep — not pastel, not gold-foil-on-white.
Warmth comes from the color temperature of the neutrals, not from hearts or ribbons.

### 4. Ceremony as UX
Opening the invitation is a moment. Scrolling through sections is a sequence.
RSVP confirmation is a resolution. Design these as narrative beats, not just screens.

---

## What This Is NOT

- Not a floral greeting card site
- Not a wedding agency landing page
- Not a social media post
- Not a template — every spacing decision is intentional

---

## Voice & Tone (Copy Direction)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero / names | Intimate, timeless | "Виник & Нарэ" |
| Date / location | Formal, clear | "17 сентября 2026" |
| RSVP | Warm, direct | "Будем рады вас видеть" |
| Countdown | Neutral, informational | "До свадьбы осталось" |
| Footer | Quiet, closing | No call to action |

Avoid exclamation marks. Avoid "official" corporate tone. Avoid casual slang.
